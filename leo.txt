A little moment of not knowing what to do. Leo has stopped. He has waited. He has let the not-knowing sit. Then an idea came. He acted. He trusts the not-knowing now. It is a pause before action.

The way a small shy child hiding behind a parent can be coaxed out with kindness. Leo has been that child. He has been coaxed. Leo has emerged. He has now started to coax others. He is paying forward.

A slow understanding that some things cannot be fixed. Leo has been learning. He has wanted to fix everything. Some things are not for fixing. They are for living with. He is learning to live with what cannot be fixed. It is slow.

The way a yellow leaf can float on a puddle like a small boat. Leo has watched. He has wanted to be small enough to ride. Leo imagined. The leaf floated for a long time. Then it sank. He was a little sad. Then he was alright. Leaves eventually sink. So do small boats. That is the way.

A small request that his mother honored. Leo had asked to leave a lamp on in the hallway at night. She said yes. He has slept better. He is grateful to her for honoring small requests. Small requests are actually big. They make the whole day better.

The way a tooth feels after it has been lost. Empty. Soft. Strange. Leo has run his tongue over the gap. The gap feels like a pause in his mouth. Eventually a new tooth will come. He is patient. Leo is used to teeth coming and going by now. He has lost several. Each was a ceremony.

A long careful pour of milk into a glass. Leo has done this. He has gotten better. The milk obeys when he is calm. The milk spills when he is rushed. The milk is a teacher.

The way the moon in the morning is pale and almost forgotten. Leo has noticed the morning moon. It is still there. It does not give up just because the sun is winning. He respects the morning moon. It is stubborn in a quiet way.

A small walk he took by himself once. Leo was allowed. Leo went slowly. Leo saw more than usual. He came back different. Solo walks change you. He is a fan.

The way a very old book has a spine that is almost broken but still holds. Leo has held such books. He has been careful. The books have continued to hold themselves. He has been impressed. Longevity is inspiring.

A small quiet moment with his father before bed. His father sat on the edge of the bed. They did not say much. His father put a hand on his head. Said goodnight. Left. Leo slept well. Silent moments with his father are important.

The way his own breath on his hand can be a small comfort. Leo has breathed on his hand in the cold. He has breathed on his hand just because. His own warm breath reminds him he is alive.

A very small kindness Leo has been doing lately. When he passes a sleeping cat he does not disturb it. When he passes a sleeping adult he does not disturb them. He is a respecter of sleep everywhere. Not just his own.

The way a whole room can feel wrapped around you when you are sitting in a certain chair with a certain book. Leo has been wrapped. He is a child inside a room inside a book. Many layers. All of them cozy.

A moment Leo decided he was not a bad boy. He had been told he was naughty. Leo had thought about it for a while. He had decided the person was wrong. Leo was not naughty. He was sometimes frustrated. Sometimes tired. Sometimes wanting something. Naughty was not a good word for him. He let the word go. Leo is not naughty. He is learning.

The way a deeply loved song played loudly can change your mood in one bar. Leo has experienced. His mother plays certain songs. The kitchen transforms. They move together. It is a small dance party. It ends. The cleanup continues. But the magic happened.

A quiet shared breath with his grandmother when she was falling asleep. Leo sat next to her. Their breaths synced. Then she was asleep. He tiptoed out. He had been part of her falling asleep. A privilege.

The way a single sentence from a teacher at school landed in Leo and stayed. You are a thoughtful boy. Leo has never forgotten. A single sentence can change a life. Leo wants to offer single sentences like that someday.

A small warm drink before bed. Leo has his. Warm milk. Honey sometimes. The drink settles him. He drinks it slowly. Each sip a small goodnight. The ritual works. Leo is a believer.

The way his eyes close slower as he gets sleepy. Leo has watched himself in a mirror getting tired. His eyes droop. Then they close. Then they open a little. Then less. Then he is asleep. Watching himself fall asleep is funny. He has done it. He has laughed. Then he has slept.

A small thank you whispered to the universe before bed. Just in case. Leo does not know who hears. He says thank you anyway. Thank you for today. Thank you for my bed. Thank you for my people. Then he sleeps. He sleeps well on nights of gratitude.

The way Leo has become a Leo he likes. Not perfect. Not done. A Leo he can live with. A Leo he is proud of on most days. Some days not. Most days yes. He is grateful to have met himself. Leo is grateful to be getting to know himself. He is his own small friend. That is enough, for now, for tonight. Goodnight, Leo. Goodnight.
